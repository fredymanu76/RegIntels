### 1.2 Board Responsibilities

The Board is collectively responsible for:
- Setting the firm's strategy and risk appetite
- Ensuring adequate financial and operational resources
- Overseeing the effectiveness of internal controls
- Maintaining appropriate governance arrangements under SM&CR
- Approving major policies and ensuring regulatory compliance
- Appointing and overseeing Senior Management Functions (SMFs)

### 1.3 Board Meetings

- The Board meets at least **quarterly**, with additional meetings as required
- A quorum requires at least 50% of directors including at least one NED
- Board papers are circulated at least **5 business days** before meetings
- Minutes are recorded and maintained for a minimum of 7 years

### 1.4 Matters Reserved for the Board

The Board retains decision-making authority over:
- Strategy and business plans
- Risk appetite and tolerance
- Capital and liquidity management
- Regulatory applications and notifications
- Appointment of SMF holders
- Annual budget and financial statements
- Significant contracts (>£500k)

## 2. Independence

### 2.1 Non-Executive Director Independence

The firm ensures a minimum of **three independent Non-Executive Directors (NEDs)** on the Board. NEDs are assessed for independence annually against the following criteria:

- No material business relationship with the firm
- No cross-directorships with executive directors
- No significant shareholding (>5%)
- Not served on the Board for more than 9 years
- No close family ties with the firm's advisers or senior employees

### 2.2 Conflicts of Interest

All directors must:
- Declare any actual or potential conflicts of interest at each Board meeting
- Maintain a register of interests, updated annually
- Recuse themselves from discussions where a conflict exists
- Seek Board approval before accepting external appointments

### 2.3 Independent Challenge

NEDs provide independent challenge through:
- Participation in all Board committees
- Access to management information and external advisers
- Regular private sessions without executive directors present
- Annual Board effectiveness review with independent facilitator

## 3. Three Lines of Defence

### 3.1 First Line — Business Operations

Business units and operational staff are responsible for:
- Identifying and managing risks within their areas
- Implementing controls and procedures
- Complying with policies and regulatory requirements
- Reporting incidents and near-misses
- Maintaining accurate records and evidence

### 3.2 Second Line — Risk and Compliance

The Risk and Compliance functions provide:
- **Risk function:** Independent risk assessment, risk framework maintenance, risk appetite monitoring, risk reporting to the Board
- **Compliance function:** Regulatory horizon scanning, compliance monitoring programme, breach management, regulatory reporting, FCA/PRA liaison

The compliance function has direct access to the Board and reports through the Risk Committee.

### 3.3 Third Line — Internal Audit

Internal audit provides independent assurance through:
- Risk-based annual audit plan approved by the Audit Committee
- Assessment of control effectiveness across all business areas
- Tracking and verification of remediation actions
- Direct reporting to the Audit Committee Chair
- Freedom from management interference in scope and findings

### 3.4 Coordination Between Lines

The three lines coordinate through:
- Joint risk assessment workshops (quarterly)
- Shared risk event and incident database
- Combined assurance mapping
- Regular trilateral meetings between Compliance, Risk, and Internal Audit

## 4. Compliance Oversight

### 4.1 Compliance Function

The Head of Compliance holds SMF16 (Compliance Oversight) and is responsible for:
- Maintaining the compliance monitoring programme
- Ensuring regulatory obligations are identified and met
- Managing the firm's relationship with the FCA
- Providing compliance advice to the Board and senior management
- Overseeing regulatory reporting and notifications

### 4.2 Compliance Monitoring Programme

The annual compliance monitoring programme covers:
- All FCA Principles for Businesses
- Key regulatory obligations under the firm's permissions
- SM&CR obligations and certification
- Consumer Duty requirements
- AML/CTF compliance
- Financial crime prevention

### 4.3 Regulatory Reporting

The firm maintains processes for:
- Timely submission of all regulatory returns
- REP-CRIM annual financial crime reporting
- REG data annual regulatory return
- Immediate notification of material breaches to the FCA
- Principal Firm reporting (where applicable)

### 4.4 Compliance Culture

The firm promotes a culture of compliance through:
- Tone from the top — Board commitment to regulatory compliance
- Training and awareness programmes for all staff
- Whistleblowing procedures with Board-level champion
- Performance management that incorporates conduct and compliance
- No tolerance for deliberate or reckless regulatory breaches
