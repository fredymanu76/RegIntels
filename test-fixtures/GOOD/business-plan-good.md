## 1. Business Model

### 1.1 Overview

The firm operates as an authorised electronic money institution (AEMI) providing:
- **E-money issuance:** Prepaid cards and digital wallets for retail and business customers
- **Payment services:** Domestic and cross-border payment transfers
- **Account information services:** Aggregated account views for business customers

### 1.2 Target Market

- **Primary:** UK-based SMEs requiring multi-currency payment capabilities
- **Secondary:** Retail consumers seeking digital wallet services
- **Geographic scope:** UK domestic with EEA cross-border passporting

### 1.3 Revenue Model

| Revenue Stream | Description | Projected Year 1 |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Transaction fees | Per-transaction fee (0.5%–1.5%) | £1.2M |
| Monthly account fees | £9.99–£49.99/month subscription | £800K |
| FX margin | Spread on currency conversion (0.5%–1.0%) | £600K |
| Interchange | Card interchange revenue | £400K |
| **Total projected revenue** | | **£3.0M** |

### 1.4 Competitive Advantage

- Proprietary payment orchestration technology
- Multi-currency IBAN issuance capability
- API-first architecture for business integration
- FCA-authorised status providing customer trust

### 1.5 Distribution Channels

- Direct-to-consumer mobile application
- B2B API integration for business customers
- Strategic partnerships with accounting software providers
- Referral programme for existing customers

## 3. Regulatory Readiness

### 3.1 FCA Authorisation Status

The firm is applying for authorisation as an Authorised Electronic Money Institution (AEMI) under the Electronic Money Regulations 2011. The application covers:

- E-money issuance (EMR Schedule 1)
- Payment services (PSD2 Schedule 1)
- Account information services (PSD2)

### 3.2 Regulatory Framework

The firm's regulatory framework includes:

- **AML/CTF Policy:** Fully developed, MLRO appointed (SMF17)
- **Safeguarding Policy:** Segregation method, accounts established with Barclays and NatWest
- **Governance Framework:** Board with 3 independent NEDs, Audit/Risk/Remuneration committees
- **Consumer Duty:** Implementation plan completed, annual assessment framework in place
- **Operational Resilience:** Important business services mapped, impact tolerances set
- **Complaints Handling:** FCA-compliant procedure, FOS membership

## 4. Capital

### 4.1 Initial Capital

The firm meets the **€350,000 initial capital requirement** for AEMI authorisation:

| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Share capital | £400,000 |
| Share premium | £600,000 |
| Retained earnings | N/A (pre-revenue) |
| **Total own funds** | **£1,000,000** |

This exceeds the €350,000 (approximately £305,000) minimum initial capital requirement.

### 4.2 Ongoing Capital Requirements

The firm will maintain own funds calculated as the higher of:
- €350,000 initial capital
- Method D calculation (2% of average outstanding e-money)

Based on Year 1 projections (average outstanding e-money of £5M):
- Method D requirement: £100,000
- Initial capital floor: £305,000
- **Own funds maintained: £1,000,000** (significant buffer)

### 4.3 Capital Monitoring

Capital adequacy is monitored:
- **Daily:** Treasury team monitors liquid assets
- **Monthly:** CFO reports capital position to Board
- **Quarterly:** Formal ICAAP-lite assessment
- **Annually:** Independent capital adequacy review

### 4.4 Capital Conservation Buffer

The firm maintains a voluntary capital conservation buffer of 25% above the minimum requirement to absorb unexpected losses and ensure continued compliance.
